# AI Wedge Positioning — Reconciled Strategy & 30-Day Plan

**Decision of record (2026-05-07, reaffirmed 2026-07-18):** AI is the visible
front door into Shank Strategy Ops. Operations is the substance underneath.
This document reconciles that decision with the July 2026 external positioning
brief, which was researched from the live site only and proposed a pure
operations/bottleneck positioning. Where the two conflict, the May decision
wins; where the brief adds conversion mechanics, we adopt them.

---

## Positioning

**Public line:** Put AI to work inside the business you already run.

**Service name:** AI-Enabled Operations Implementation

**Audience:** Small business owners, founders, and team leads who know AI
should be taking work off their plate and can't get it past the demo stage —
plus the original audience: leaders losing momentum to unclear ownership, slow
decisions, and execution drift. AI is the easier door; both walk into the same
practice.

**Voice discipline (unchanged):**
- Lead with relief, not technology.
- Refuse "AI transformation" by name. Sell one workflow that works.
- Qualification, scope control, and authority checks live inside delivery,
  not at first contact.

## Naming resolution (was an open question since May)

**One offer, two-layer naming.** "Operator Audit" stays the proper product
name — it is indexed, has schema.org Service markup, and carries the site's
existing search equity. "The AI workflow reality check" is its public
descriptor (page eyebrow, schema `alternateName`, homepage framing). No
parallel offers; one front door.

## Offer ladder (public names, gated pricing)

| Rung | Offer | Pricing |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Operator Audit (the AI workflow reality check) | $500, public |
| 2 | AI Workflow Sprint | HELD — "scoped from the audit" |
| 3 | AI Operations Buildout | HELD — "scoped from the audit" |
| 4 | AI Operator Retainer | HELD — "by fit only" |

L2–L4 pricing stays unpublished until the first $500 audit closes (May gate,
still active). The site shows the ladder's names and sequence only.

## Messaging pillars (brief's pillars, AI-wedge framed)

1. **Find the actual bottleneck** — including what AI is really doing in the
   operation today: helping, pretending to help, or making it worse.
2. **One workflow that works** — not a transformation program. Prove it under
   live conditions, then move to the next.
3. **Restore clarity, time, and trust** — kept verbatim; it names what buyers
   feel.
4. **Durable systems without enterprise theater** — cadence, ownership,
   decision rights sized for small teams.
5. **Start bounded, then go deeper** — the $500 audit is the safest first
   step and complete on its own.

## Risks and gates

- **Proof gap:** no public case evidence yet — claims stay measured until the
  first audits produce anonymizable examples.
- **Wedge without substance:** if AI-led copy attracts buyers wanting a tool
  install, the audit's diagnostic framing is the filter — it prices and
  scopes reality first.
- **Held gates:** L2–L4 pricing and any "transformation"-shaped engagement
  language remain blocked until the audit motion is proven.
