## Post 2 — Bottleneck of the Week (diagnostic/teaching)

**Publish:** Day 3–4. Establishes the recurring format.

> Bottleneck of the week: the report that reads clean and decides
> nothing.
>
> A pattern I keep seeing in small teams (a pattern, not any one
> client):
>
> Someone drafts the weekly update with AI. It's well-written.
> Confident tone, complete sentences, tidy summary at the end.
>
> And it doesn't decide anything.
>
> The owner reads it, feels informed, and the actual question — who
> does what by when — is still open. Work stays "in motion" without
> moving.
>
> That's not an AI problem. It's an ownership problem AI made easier
> to hide. The drafting got faster; the deciding didn't.
>
> The check I use:
>
> — Every recurring doc names an owner and a next decision, or it's
> noise.
> — If AI wrote it, a person is still accountable for it. "The tool
> said so" is not an owner.
> — If a meeting reviews the doc and nothing changes hands, cut the
> doc or cut the meeting.
>
> AI should take work off your plate: drafting, summarizing, triaging.
> It cannot take ownership off your plate. Most "AI isn't helping us"
> complaints are that confusion wearing a trench coat.
>
> Where in your operation does the work read clean and decide nothing?
